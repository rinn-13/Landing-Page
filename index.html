Origin

The King of Heroes

Gilgamesh is the legendary demigod who ruled the ancient city of Uruk — humanity's greatest hero and its most arrogant king. Born of two-thirds divine blood and one-third mortal, he ruled not through compassion, but through absolute dominance.

His treasury, the Gate of Babylon, holds the original prototype of every treasure ever created by humanity — for all things in this world were his before they were anyone else's. His crimson eyes, Sha Naqba Imuru, perceive the very depths of all existence.

He despises the mediocrity of modern man, yet silently watches with the eyes of a king who understands — better than anyone — what it means to lose something irreplaceable.

Incarnations of the King

Gilgamesh is the only Heroic Spirit capable of being summoned into multiple distinct classes. Each incarnation reveals a different facet of this perfect yet contradictory king.

The most iconic and most arrogant incarnation of Gilgamesh. As Archer, he presents himself as a king who looks down upon all humans and fellow Servants alike. He is not a true Archer in the traditional sense — he possesses no exceptional archery skill — yet his Gate of Babylon, which fires treasures as projectiles, makes him a natural fit for the class.

First appearing in Fate/Zero, Gilgamesh Archer was preserved by Kotomine Kirei and resurfaces in Fate/Stay Night as the primary antagonist. He is the most dangerous enemy not because of raw power alone, but because he possesses every Noble Phantasm as a "key" within his Gate of Babylon.

Gilgamesh Caster is the wisest incarnation of the king — a ruler who truly governs his people, fighting to protect humanity against the Three Goddesses Alliance in the Babylonia singularity. Unlike the cold and arrogant Archer, Caster Gil reveals the qualities of a genuine leader.

In Fate/Grand Order: Babylonia, he welcomes Fujimaru as the one Master he summoned from the future. He knows his fate — that he will fall — yet continues to stand and rule Uruk until the very last moment. This is the Gilgamesh known from the original Mesopotamian epic: the king who learned the meaning of life after the death of Enkidu.

Caster Gil is one of the strongest Support Servants in FGO, owing to his powerful Arts card buffs and exceptional NP. He provides ATK buffs, NP generation, and a wide array of support effects for all allies.

Disposition
Childish · Yet Kingly

The child version of Gilgamesh — more precisely, the adolescent form of the King of Uruk — first appeared in Fate/kaleid liner Prisma Illya. Despite his small frame, his arrogant and imperious personality remains entirely intact. He still considers himself a king and demands the same reverence.

In Fate/Grand Order, he is categorized as a Archer and represents a "younger" Gilgamesh — both physically and emotionally. He is less restrained and more openly expressive of his desires, albeit in a manner that is simultaneously childish and imperious.

Though his capabilities do not match the adult Archer version, Child Gilgamesh still has access to the Gate of Babylon and Ea in a limited form. Many fans refer to this version as "Gil-kun" — a name he would despise immensely if he ever heard it.
